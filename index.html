S Orbitals

S orbitals are perfectly spherical electron clouds centered on the nucleus. They represent the most fundamental orbital type, with electron probability symmetrically distributed in all directions.

Quantum Number l = 0

Perfectly Spherical Geometry

Electron Probability: Highest at Nucleus

Maximum of 2 Electrons

P Orbitals

P orbitals are characterized by their distinctive dumbbell shape, existing in three orthogonal orientations (px, py, pz). They represent higher energy electron configurations with lobes extending along cardinal axes.

Quantum Number l = 1

Three Orthogonal Orientations

Directional Electron Probability

Maximum of 6 Electrons

D Orbitals

D orbitals introduce more complex geometries with five distinct spatial orientations. They play a crucial role in transition metal chemistry, creating intricate electron distribution patterns that enable unique bonding characteristics.

Quantum Number l = 2

Five Unique Spatial Configurations

Critical in Transition Metal Chemistry

Maximum of 10 Electrons

F Orbitals

F orbitals represent the most complex electron configurations in the standard atomic model. With seven distinct spatial orientations, they are predominantly found in lanthanide and actinide series, playing a critical role in the chemistry of rare earth and radioactive elements.

Quantum Number l = 3

Seven Complex Spatial Configurations

Dominant in Rare Earth Elements

Maximum of 14 Electrons
