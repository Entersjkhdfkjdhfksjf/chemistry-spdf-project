Atomic Orbitals
S Orbitals P Orbitals D Orbitals F Orbitals

S Orbitals

The S orbital has a spherical shape with no angular nodes. It represents the simplest electron distribution with a maximum of two electrons.

P Orbitals

The P orbitals are shaped like dumbbells and can be oriented along three axes (x, y, z). Each P orbital can hold up to six electrons in total.

D Orbitals

The D orbitals have more complex shapes, typically described as cloverleafs. There are five D orbitals in total, each with different orientations.

F Orbitals

The F orbitals have even more complex shapes, with seven lobes in total. These orbitals play an important role in the chemistry of lanthanides and actinides.
